# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI Fraud

---

### 🧑‍🎓 What the crime is

Riya is a first-year college student who received a phone call from a person who claimed to be a representative of the bank. This person told her that there is a problem with her account and that it needed verification. Since the situation is serious, she trusted the person and did accordingly.

---

### ⚠️ How the Fraud Happened

The first thing the scammer did was to ask her to verify her details to gain her trust. Then, he sent her a "collect request" on her UPI app. He told her that in order to receive a refund, she had to approve the request. Not knowing much about the process, Riya approved the request and put in her UPI PIN. Immediately, money was deducted from her account.

---

### 🔍 What Type of Crime Is This?

This type of cybercrime is called "UPI or online payment fraud." Usually, in this type of fraud, the person creating the account or making the payment tries to create a sense of urgency and pretend to be a trusted authority in order to trick the victim.

---

### 🎯 Who Is Targeted?

This type of fraud targets students or young individuals because they are more likely to use online payment apps. They are not yet aware of these types of scams.

---

### ⚡ Consequences

The consequences of UPI frauds like these include financial loss, stress, and a loss of trust in digital payment systems.
